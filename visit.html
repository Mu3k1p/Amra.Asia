How to get here

Koh Dach sits in the Mekong River, about 15km north of central Phnom Penh. You'll cross by ferry — it's part of the experience.

About Koh Dach

Silk Island

Koh Dach (Silk Island) — Mekong River, Phnom Penh

Known as Silk Island for its centuries-old weaving tradition, Koh Dach is a peaceful escape from the city. Rice paddies, bamboo huts, riverside sunsets — and now, Amra waiting at the end of your ride.

Step-by-step directions
01
From central Phnom Penh, head north across the Japanese Bridge. About 12–15km — around 30 minutes by tuk-tuk or taxi, roughly $5–10.
02
Take the ferry to Koh Dach. Runs 8am–8pm daily. Costs 1,000 riels per person (~$0.25), or 3,000 riels if you bring a motorbike.
03
On the island, Amra is roadside on the Koh Dach Track. Large parking area out front. Look for the retro-style building surrounded by greenery.
Tip
Best visited December through February. Rent a bicycle or scooter (~$5/day) and pair your café stop with the nearby silk weaving center.
